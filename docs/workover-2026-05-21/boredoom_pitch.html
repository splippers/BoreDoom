Overview

What is BoreDOOM! ?

BoreDOOM! is a Meta Quest 3 augmented reality game that transforms your home into a game level. When you pick up the hoover, mow the lawn, or tidy the shed, BoreDOOM! turns the chore into a competitive, score-based experience — tracking your real physical movements and visualising them as gameplay inside your actual room.

The core loop is simple: move through real space → see real-time AR feedback → score points → beat your ghost → share the result. The dopamine hit of watching your house turn green in the dollhouse view is genuinely addictive.

The Viral Hook
At the end of every session, BoreDOOM! generates a 1080×1080 share card: an overhead view of your miniature dollhouse with coverage heat-maps, your grade, and your score. It looks insane. People post it. Their friends want to try it. The game sells itself.

The Dollhouse System

Your home is mirrored as a 1:20-scale 3D dollhouse rendered in the corner of your passthrough view. Rooms update in real-time as you clean them — dirty red → in-progress amber → clean green → pristine cyan. When every room hits Pristine, the app auto-generates your share card.

The Ghost Run System

Every session is saved as a ghost: a path through your home with timing data. Next time you do the same chore, your ghost replays as a translucent blue line and floating head. Beat it and you set a new personal best. It's Strava for housework.

How It Works (Technical)

  • Passthrough AR: Meta Quest 3 colour passthrough — your real room, not a virtual one.
  • Spatial Mesh: OpenXR mesh subsystem reads the room geometry. Coverage is tracked on a 25cm-cell grid mapped to real floor area.
  • OVR Scene API: Room boundaries, floor area, and object classifications come from Meta's scene understanding.
  • Path Tracker: Controller position is sampled 10×/sec and stored as a world-space vector list for ghost replay and coverage calculation.
  • Unity MCP: AI agents can interact with the Unity Editor directly via the MCP plugin for rapid asset wiring.

Zone Modes

Special Zone Modes

These modes target specific high-chaos rooms in the home. Each inherits full coverage tracking but adds a unique twist mechanic that rewards thorough, methodical cleaning.

Attic Attack
— "Fear the dark. Clean it anyway."
ZONE

Attic cleaning in near-darkness — only the "flashlight cone" in front of you counts for coverage. Cobweb zones require 2-second dwell to "clear." Skip more than 20% of cobwebs and a Darkness Penalty (0.8×) hits your final score. Looking up at the ceiling earns double points.

Unique Mechanics
  • Flashlight coverage cone
  • Cobweb dwell zones (8 total)
  • Ceiling scan bonus
  • Darkness penalty
Bonus Points
  • +200 max ceiling bonus
  • +150 cobweb clear bonus
  • 0.8× multiplier if <80% cobwebs
Shed Dread
— "Spiders. Tools. Respect."
ZONE

Organise a chaotic shed. 10 AR tool icons float around — pick each one up (1.5s dwell) and file it in the correct colour-coded zone. Correct placement builds a combo multiplier. Misplacements cost 20 pts. Random spider encounters spawn for bonus points if you "shoo" them away.

Unique Mechanics
  • Tool pickup + placement system
  • 6 colour-coded storage zones
  • Combo multiplier for streaks
  • Random spider encounters
Scoring
  • 50 pts × combo per correct place
  • −20 pts per misplacement
  • +10 pts per spider shooed
  • Coverage still tracked normally

Base Score Formula

Base Points = (coverage_percent × 10) + (efficiency_score × 5) + (movement_score × 5)
Total Points = Base × power_up_multiplier + bonus_points

Bonus points from mode-specific mechanics are added before grading, so they can push you into S+ territory.

Technical Architecture

DollhouseManager

  • Manages room state dictionary
  • Animated material transitions (0.8s smooth lerp)
  • WorldToDollhouse(pos) — 1:20 scale conversion
  • Auto-triggers share card when all rooms Pristine
  • CraicKen reporter integration

DollhouseRoomSlicer

  • Reads OVRSceneRoom anchors on Quest 3
  • Creates scaled floor quads per room
  • Editor placeholder (5×5m) for testing
  • Feeds Renderer[] into DollhouseManager
  • ApplyCoverageToRoom() hook

DollhouseHoverTrail

  • LineRenderer on the dollhouse
  • Maps PathTracker positions via WorldToDollhouse
  • Height locked to 0.01f (just above floor)
  • Updated every LateUpdate when new points arrive

DollhouseSnapshotCamera

  • Orthographic camera above the dollhouse
  • 1080×1080 RenderTexture → PNG
  • Saved to persistent data path
  • Android: MediaScanner makes it gallery-visible

Viral Potential

Why This Goes Viral

01
The Share Card
Every session ends with an auto-generated overhead shot of your dollhouse — heat-mapped, graded, stamped. It's visually distinctive and instantly shows your achievement. People post it without being asked.
02
Mowing Art
Tracing geometric patterns in your lawn or carpet, then capturing the overhead dollhouse view, creates content that looks like crop circles. The TikTok category writes itself: "I traced a diamond in my living room."
03
Chaos Assessment
Show people their chaos score. Everyone is either insulted by their result or proud of it. "My living room scored 8.7/10 DOOMED 🔥" is a shareable sentence. The roast comments write themselves.
04
ChoreWars Battle
Two people in different homes racing to clean simultaneously, with a live score ticker, creates urgency and stakes. "My partner beat me to 80% while I was still finding the hoover" = genuine story content.
05
Ghost Run
Chasing your own ghost while hoovering is genuinely funny to explain. "I'm racing myself. My past self hoovered faster." The concept is absurd in the best way. Fitness apps wish they could claim this energy.
06
The Dollhouse Moment
Watching your real house render in miniature in the corner of your AR view while you clean it — rooms turning from red to green in real time — is a profound enough moment that first-timers describe it as "magic." That word travels.
Target Market

Primary: 18–35 Quest 3 owners who already exercise with VR fitness apps (Beat Saber, Supernatural). Secondary: Productivity-gamification audience (Habitica, BeReal). The key insight: these people already own the hardware and already feel guilty about their messy homes. BoreDOOM! is the app that resolves that cognitive dissonance.
